The Internet has connected the world in ways we once only dreamed of, enabling collaboration, business, and endless fun on a global scale. With just a browser and a URL, you can share a story that spreads like wildfire, buy that must-have gadget, or lose yourself in the latest hit song.

But wait, there’s more! This digital dance party has room for improvement. *What if we told you that a barrier exists that needs conquering? That barrier?* **Languages.**

For English speakers, the Internet is a comfortable realm, almost an extended living room. But we’re on a mission to make that comfort universal, to extend the invite to every corner of the globe. Think of someone who might struggle with English-centric content. This isn’t just a call to arms for those who face language barriers; it’s a shout-out to everyone who loves innovation, including English speakers. Together, we can crank up the volume on a multi-lingual Internet, where platforms like Web, iOS, and Android can groove without limits.

Now, you might wonder why we aren’t there yet. Our time at [Shopify](https://shopify.com) revealed a complex puzzle, one with pieces scattered across the floor. Picture this: *The world of localization wrestling with software’s best practices, translation delays causing continuity hiccups, stakeholders and developers struggling to merge translation PRs, and more.* It’s a scene full of barriers, and we want to tear them down.

We’re not just talking about technical issues here. The bigger problem lies in **the attitudes and perspectives that shape the translation and localization industry.** When executives of organizations only speak one language, they might overlook the immense value in linguistic accessibility. But we at Glossia see it differently. We believe that breaking down language barriers will enrich products, spur innovation, and foster positive growth.

Let’s take a quick stroll down Memory Lane. The **Internet handed the localization industry a golden opportunity to revisit old ideas and build something grand.** But, unfortunately, the same ideas kept cropping up with only minor tweaks. What’s been missing is innovation, a willingness to **rethink the whole system** from the ground up.

Software, on the other hand, embraced a new era of openness and collaboration. Remember when [Linux](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linux) opened its source code, enabling global contributions? That spark ignited a wave of innovation and creativity that we’re still riding today. Can you believe Microsoft’s transformation into a massive advocate for open-source, or Linus Torvald’s ground-breaking [Git](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Git)? These leaps **catapulted software into a realm where innovation thrived.**

Localization could’ve taken a similar path, but it didn’t. Fear of idea theft, complex systems, and hesitance to change kept it shackled to the old ways.

*But what if we could change that? What if we designed mental models and systems that sparked a localization renaissance?* It’s not an easy task, but it’s an exhilarating challenge that could lead to more efficient and creative localization.

Here comes Glossia, blending AI with radically different interfaces, workflows, and experiences. We believe in translating fast without compromising quality, automatically translating with AI that gets fine-tuned over time. *Worried about low quality?* Glossia’s automated translations are like fine wine; **they get better with age.** And that’s an attractive proposition for organizations.

Traditional translation memories had limitations, but Glossia’s **concurrency-proof models** transform translation into a flexible and evolving process. We’re empowering translators with new creative roles, supported by **Glossia as a copilot.** Imagine marketers and CEOs shaping content with a click, or models that adapt to specific tones and acronyms. The future is linguistically vibrant, and it starts with Glossia.

What’s more, we’re building Glossia as **an extensible platform** that integrates seamlessly into existing workflows. Developers will have the tools they need to localize digital experiences, foster innovation, and break free from the closed-off ecosystem.

We’re all about **openness** at Glossia. Transparency, open-source, community vibe – we’re laying it all on the table. Our long-term goal is to open source Glossia, create a thriving ecosystem, and keep you in the loop every step of the way.
